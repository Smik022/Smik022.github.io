ZeroTouch Onboarding

Product Manager•Femia Accountants

Client onboarding for an Australian accounting firm, built against a hard AML/CTF deadline — quote, intake, due diligence, and write-back into the firm's practice-management system.

Situation

From 1 July 2026, Australian AML/CTF rules required Femia Accountants to identify, verify and prove every client. The principal was running those checks by hand, across three separate Excel quote calculators and paper due-diligence forms. The deadline was not negotiable, and the evidence trail had to survive an audit.

Task

  • Turn the firm's manual quote calculators and AML due-diligence forms into one system, before the compliance date.
  • Keep the firm's pricing private from clients — a hard requirement that shaped the whole flow.
  • Write every ID and AML check back into Karbon, the firm's practice-management system, so the evidence lives where auditors look for it.

Result

A working system covering quote → client intake → AML record → write-back, proven end to end against the firm's live Karbon, Microsoft Graph and Stripe Identity tenants. At last commit it was feature-complete on staging; production infrastructure and per-user SSO are the remaining gates before real client data.

My Contribution

  • Split the flow into two forms — staff-facing with pricing, client-facing without — once the firm made clear that clients must never see the price grid. That single constraint drove the entire information architecture.
  • Ruled that dry-run and live must share one code path. Karbon writes are permanent and have no delete, so what we tested had to be exactly what fired; a flag decides whether the call goes out, nothing else changes.
  • Caught that Karbon's Director ID field is number-typed, which silently drops the leading zero from Australian Director IDs beginning '036'. Rather than store a corrupted compliance identifier, we skip the field and write the ID verbatim into the AML timeline note.
  • Held every integration awaiting a client decision as a dormant adapter — fully built, returning 'not configured', sending nothing externally until switched on. No client name leaves the server before the firm says so.
  • Reconciled the principal's master pricing workbook live from SharePoint, surfacing stale fees for one-click review instead of asking him to re-key them.

Key Results

576 Row business price matrix

Generated directly from the firm's own Excel quote builder rather than transcribed by hand, so the source of truth stays with the client.

Three quote engines Individual ITR, SMSF, Business

Classify-then-price: tick the activities, land on exactly one return type by precedence, then stack schedules for CGT, dividends, foreign income and rentals.

AML/CTF due diligence Individual, Company, Trust

The firm's paper forms digitised into a register with risk, PEP status and CDD level, plus one-click document export and an automatic timeline note against the client record.

269 assertions 14 harnesses, no test framework

The pricing engines are the part a client would notice being wrong, so they get a regression net that transpiles the TypeScript in-process — no test dependency to install or maintain.
